## Number-Based Problems

1. **Armstrong Number**  
   Sum of digits raised to the power of number of digits equals the number.  
   Example: `153 → 1³ + 5³ + 3³ = 153`

2. **Palindrome Number**  
   Number reads the same forward and backward.  
   Example: `121`

3. **Reverse a Number**  
   Reverse the digits of a number.  
   Example: `123 → 321`

4. **Sum of Digits**  
   Add all digits of a number.  
   Example: `456 → 15`

5. **Product of Digits**  
   Multiply all digits of a number.  
   Example: `123 → 6`

6. **Count Digits**  
   Count the total digits in a number.  
   Example: `7890 → 4 digits`

7. **Strong Number**  
   Sum of factorial of digits equals the number.  
   Example: `145`

8. **Harshad Number (Niven Number)**  
   Number divisible by the sum of its digits.  
   Example: `18`

9. **Spy Number**  
   Sum of digits equals product of digits.  
   Example: `1124`

10. **Neon Number**  
    Sum of digits of the square equals the number.  
    Example: `9 → 9² = 81 → 8 + 1 = 9`

11. **Perfect Number**  
    Sum of proper divisors equals the number.  
    Example: `6`

12. **Abundant Number**  
    Sum of divisors is greater than the number.  
    Example: `12`

13. **Deficient Number**  
    Sum of divisors is less than the number.  
    Example: `8`

14. **Prime Number**  
    Number having exactly two factors.  
    Example: `7`

15. **Composite Number**  
    Number having more than two factors.  
    Example: `10`

16. **Find All Factors**  
    Print all divisors of a number.  
    Example: `12 → 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12`

17. **Count Factors**  
    Count the number of divisors of a number.  
    Example: `16`

18. **Fibonacci Series**  
    Each number is the sum of the previous two numbers.  
    Example: `0 1 1 2 3 ...`

19. **Factorial**  
    Multiply numbers from 1 to N.  
    Example: `5! = 120`

20. **Automorphic Number**  
    Square of the number ends with the same number.  
    Example: `25 → 25² = 625`

21. **Duck Number**  
    Contains zero but not at the beginning.  
    Example: `102`

22. **Disarium Number**  
    Sum of digits powered by their positions equals the number.  
    Example: `135`

23. **Happy Number**  
    Repeatedly replace the number by the sum of squares of digits until it becomes 1.  
    Example: `19`

24. **Buzz Number**  
    Number divisible by 7 or ending with 7.  
    Example: `14`
